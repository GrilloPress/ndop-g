1. Overview

Your health data is very important to the NHS. It is shared between doctors, hospitals and dentists for reasons linked to your individual care.

Your personally identifiable information is also shared beyond your individual care for research and planning purposes. This helps to improve health and social care services for you and your family. The NHS depends on this information to plan essential facilities, like heart units and specialist care wards. The data can also be used in research to improve treatment for serious conditions and disease.

Benefits of data sharing

Improve individual care

Learn more about diseases

Make quicker diagnoses

Develop new treatments

Prevent serious illness

Plan NHS health services

Improve patient safety

Evaluate NHS policy

Reduce length of stay

Registering your preferences

You now have the choice to opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable information for purposes beyond your individual care. This means it will not be shared for reasons linked to research or planning.

Use this online service to register your preferences for sharing your patient data. By sharing your data you can contribute to the health of future generations.
