Overview

Whenever you visit an NHS service, care professionals must keep records about you. These may be electronic, paper or a mixture of both. Your records contain your health and social care information.

The NHS may share this personally identifiable information in two different ways. The first reason for sharing is for your own individual care. This allows healthcare professionals, like doctors and dentists, to see your important medical details.

The second reason for sharing is to help the NHS research, plan and improve health and social care services for you and your family.

Summary of data use

Who uses your data?

NHS Digital collects data from a wide variety of NHS trusts, local authorities and independent sector organisations. Certain institutes can request access to this health and care data.

What are the benefits of sharing data?

Patient data can be used in research to improve treatment for serious illnesses and conditions as well as managing and preparing health and social care services in England.

Find out more

Where does your opt-out apply?

If you opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable information, details like your name and address will no longer appear in data shared by NHS Digital.

There are some circumstances where opt-outs do not apply, and your personally identifiable data will still be used.

Find out more

How the opt-out process works

1. Check your details

You enter your details and we look for your contact details in the NHS England patient database.

2. Confirm your identity

We send you a verification code to your chosen contact method, which you enter online.

3. Set your preferences

You confirm your data sharing preferences, which will be applied within 21 days.

Find out more

Registering a preference

If you do not wish to share your patient data, you can use our online service to request that your personally identifiable information is not used or shared beyond your own individual care.
