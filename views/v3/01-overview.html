Overview

Whenever you visit an NHS service, care professionals must keep records about you. These may be electronic, paper or a mixture of both. Your records contain your health and social care information.

The NHS may share this personally identifiable information in two different ways. The first reason for sharing is for your own individual care. This allows healthcare professionals, like doctors and dentists, to see your important medical details.

Secondary purposes

The second reason for sharing is to help the NHS research, plan and improve health and social care services for you and your family.

This helps the NHS:

  • plan essential facilities like heart units
  • understand the need for specialist care wards
  • research potential cures for life-threatening diseases
  • improve treatment for serious illnesses

Your preferences

By sharing your data you can contribute to the health of future generations. However, if you don't agree with sharing your data for secondary purposes, you can opt-out of this. This will not affect your individual care. Register your preference now or continue reading for more information.

Where does your opt-out apply?

If you opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable information, details like your name and address will no longer appear in data shared by NHS Digital. This will only happen if opt-outs apply to the data set being shared. There are some circumstances where opt-outs do not apply, and your personally identifiable data will still be used.

Where opt-outs do not apply

An opt-out will not apply:

  • Where the information is used for purposes relating to your individual care
  • Where the personal confidential information held by NHS Digital does not contain an NHS number
  • If you have given consent for your data to be used for a specific reason, like a medical research study
  • If the information is not classed as personal confidential information. This is a combination of data that identifies who you are and information on your health, care or treatment. Personal confidential information is defined in the NHS Act 2006, Section 251, paragraphs 10 and 11
  • Where anonymised data means individuals are not identifiable from the information
  • Where there is a legal requirement to provide information, such as an order of court
  • Where there is an overriding public interest in the disclosure of your data
  • Where information is used to support the management of communicable diseases and other risks to public health. This comes under Regulation 3 of the Health Service (Control of Patient Information) Regulations 2002
  • Where birth and cancer information is supplied to the Office for National Statistics

How the opt-out process works

1. Check your details

You enter your details for us to check against the NHS England patient database. This allows us to check whether you have a mobile number or email address stored against your record.

2. Confirm your identity

We send you a verification code to your chosen contact method. You enter this code online so that we know it's really you setting your opt-out preferences.

3. Set your preferences

Once you have confirmed who you are, you can set and save your data sharing preferences. These preferences will be applied within 21 days of being set.

Registering a preference

If you do not wish to share your patient data, you can use our online service to request that your personally identifiable information is not used or shared beyond your own individual care.

Your choice will be respected by all organisations that use health and social care information. This does not include if there is a legal reason or obligation to use your information.

Your decision will not affect your own individual care in any way. You can also change your mind and set a different preference in the future, if you wish to do so.
