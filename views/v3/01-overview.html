Overview

Whenever you visit an NHS service, care professionals must keep records about you. These may be electronic, paper or a mixture of both. Your records contain your health and social care information.

Who uses your data?

the NHS collects data from a wide variety of NHS trusts, local authorities and independent sector organisations. Certain institutes can request access to this health and care data.

What are the benefits of sharing data?

Patient data can be used in research to improve treatment for serious illnesses and conditions as well as managing and preparing health and social care services in England.

Find out more

Where does your opt-out apply?

If you do not want your data to be used in this way you can opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable information, meaning details like your name and address will no longer appear in data shared by the NHS.

There are some circumstances where opt-outs do not apply, and your personally identifiable data will still be used.

Find out more

How the opt-out process works

1.

Check your details

You enter your details and we look for your contact information in the NHS England patient database.

2.

Confirm your identity

We send a verification code to your selected contact method and you enter this online.

3.

Set your preferences

You confirm your data sharing preferences, which will be applied within 21 days.

Register a preference

You can use our online service to request that your personally identifiable data information is not used or shared beyond your own individual care.
