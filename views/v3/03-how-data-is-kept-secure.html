The NHS takes the responsibility for protecting your personally identifiable data very seriously. Information Governance (IG) across the NHS ensures your data is looked after in accordance with good practice and the law. If an organisation canâ€™t prove it will look after data to IG standards, their request for access will be rejected.

Those who have access to data must have appropriate training. They must also be approved by the organisation requesting access.

To ensure your data is secure:

  • technology is used to restrict access and encrypt data
  • information can only be read with a code
  • national IT systems are kept up-to-date and relevant
  • audit trails record every instance that data is viewed or used

Our pledge

When using your data for improving health and care services, we pledge to:

  • take every step to keep data secure
  • use anonymised data whenever we can
  • only use personally identifiable data to benefit health and care
  • use the minimum information where possible e.g. removing your name and using your NHS number instead
  • give you a choice about sharing personally identifiable data whenever we can
  • respect your decision if you opt out of personally identifiable data being shared beyond your individual care
  • never use or share personally identifiable data for marketing or insurance purposes (unless you specifically tell us that you want us to)
  • only share information about you where allowed by the law and where there has been independent scrutiny and approval
  • make it clear on our website what we use data for, who we share information with and why

Secure IT systems

the NHS provide secure IT systems for use across the NHS. These secure IT systems meet stringent rules and legal regulations. They are kept up-to-date and relevant to protect against viruses and new cyber threats. This ensures we follow good practice when looking after sensitive information.
