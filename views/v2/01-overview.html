1. {{content_title}}

Whenever you visit an NHS service, care professionals must keep records about you. These may be electronic, paper or a mixture of both. Your records contain your health and social care information.

Use this online service to register your preferences for sharing your patient data.

Who uses your data

the NHS collects data from a wide variety of NHS trusts, local authorities and independent sector organisations.

Certain institutes can request access to this health and care data.

If released, this information must be used to support the delivery of health and social care services in England. The request will be rejected if this is not the case.

Third party marketing or insurance companies will not be given access to your data.

How data is kept secure

The NHS takes the responsibility for protecting your personally identifiable data very seriously. Information Governance (IG) across the NHS ensures your data is looked after in accordance with good practice and the law.

The benefits of data sharing

The NHS may share this personally identifiable information in two different ways. The first reason for sharing is for your own individual care. This allows healthcare professionals, like doctors and dentists, to see your important medical details.

The second reason for sharing is to help the NHS research, plan and improve health and social care services for you and your family.

This helps the NHS:

  • plan essential facilities like heart units
  • understand the need for specialist care wards
  • research potential cures for life-threatening diseases
  • improve treatment for serious illnesses
Find out more

How opting out works

If you opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable information, details like your name and address will no longer appear in data shared by the NHS.

This will only happen if opt-outs apply to the data set being shared.

Once an opt-out request is received, it can take up to 21 days before it can be applied to future data releases.

Find out more

Register your preference

By sharing your data you can contribute to the health of future generations. However, if you don't agree with sharing your data for secondary purposes, you can opt-out of this.

This will not affect your individual care.
